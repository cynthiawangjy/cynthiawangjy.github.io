MyFreelance — Design Challenge

Empowering freelancers to manage their businesses with greater control

In response to a design challenge, I designed a high-fidelity dashboard over two days to help freelancers take control of their businesses. The project tested my ability to balance visual craft with user-centered thinking under pressure.

My submission advanced me to the remaining interview rounds and ultimately landed me the role.

TIMELINE 2 days March 2025
ROLE Product Designer Visual Design, Product Strategy
FOR Design Challenge

THE BRIEF

Design a business management dashboard for freelancers.

When using the product, users must understand:

1.

How is my business doing financially?

2.

How should I plan my future work?

3.

What can I do now to move my business forward?

IDENTIFYING THE AUDIENCE

Understanding the users

Freelancers come in all shapes and sizes, so I had to keep in mind:

Profession

Designers, developers, writers, photographers, marketers, and more

Work arrangement

Some freelance full-time, others part-time, some have retainer contracts

Experience level

Brand new freelancers vs. seasoned professionals have different needs

PROCESS

Translating needs into design opportunities

I started by breaking down each question from the brief and thinking about what that actually means from a user and design perspective.

MY GOALS

Within the limited time, I focused on three key priorities:

Information at a glance

Display critical business metrics upfront so users can quickly assess their situation without digging through menus

Flexibility for all experience levels

Accommodate both new freelancers who need structure and guidance, and seasoned professionals who need customization and advanced features

Community-driven growth

Enable referrals and connections between freelancers to grow collaboratively rather than in isolation

IDENTITY

Building the visual foundation

Typography

I chose Inter for body text to keep the interface clean and highly readable, with Rethink Sans used sparingly for the logo to add personality without overwhelming the functional aesthetic.

Colors

I chose warmer background colors to make the product feel more approachable and friendly. I also incorporated semantic colors to convey meaning at a glance, effective for skimming.

THE SOLUTION

Introducing MyFreelance

01 / LAYOUT

See your financial health at a glance and stay on top of what's happening

Freelancers juggle projects, clients, and finances. The dashboard surfaces essentials — active work, earnings, and tasks — so users instantly see how their business is doing without digging through menus.

02 / COLLABORATION MANAGEMENT

Simplifying client requests

Clicking a request opens a side panel with project details, client info, and a proposed budget. Users must open the details before being able to Accept, Refer, or Decline — intentional friction that encourages thoughtful action.

The Refer option builds community by letting freelancers pass along projects they can’t take, building trust and reciprocity within their network.

03 / PERSONAL TASK MANAGEMENT

Flexible views for different work styles

A simple toggle switches between List View for detailed sorting and Calendar View for a visual schedule, supporting workflow preferences and keeping tasks manageable.

OPPORTUNITIES

Areas for expansion

Beyond the dashboard, I identified several opportunities to increase user value and business viability:

Information at a glance

for the business and users

Allow freelancers to create public profiles where clients can book them directly, transforming MyFreelance from a management tool into a two-way marketplace

Monetization strategies

for the business

Subscription tiers to provide access to curated client networks, mentorship programs for skill development, and training modules on business growth and workflow optimization

A simple time tracking

for users

A one-click timer to track billable hours in real-time, addressing the common pain point of losing track of project time
