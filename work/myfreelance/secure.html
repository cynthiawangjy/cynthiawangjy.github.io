MyFreelance — Design Challenge

Empowering freelancers to manage their businesses with greater control

In response to a design challenge, I designed a dashboard to help freelancers take control of their businesses. My work advanced me to the remaining interview rounds, which ultimately landed me the role.

TIMELINE 2 days March 2025
ROLE Product Designer Visual Design, Product Strategy
FOR Design Challenge

THE CHALLENGE

Design a business management dashboard for freelancers.

When using the product, users must understand:

1.

How is my business doing financially?

2.

How should I plan my future work?

3.

What can I do now to move my business forward?

IDENTIFYING THE AUDIENCE

Understanding the users

Freelancers come in all shapes and sizes, so I had to keep in mind:

Profession

Designers, developers, writers, photographers, marketers, and more

Work arrangement

Some freelance full-time, others part-time, some have retainer contracts

Experience level

Brand new freelancers vs. seasoned professionals have different needs

THE PROCESS

Translating needs into design opportunities

I started by breaking down each question from the brief and thinking about what that actually means from a user and design perspective.

GUIDING PRINCIPLES

Within the limited time, I focused on three key priorities:

1.

Information at a glance

Display critical business metrics upfront so users can quickly assess their situation without digging through menus

2.

Tailored to all experience levels

Accommodate both new freelancers who need structure and guidance, and seasoned professionals who need customization and advanced features

3.

Community-driven growth

Enable referrals and connections between freelancers to grow collaboratively rather than in isolation

THE IDENTITY

Building the visual foundation

Typography

I chose Inter for body text to keep the interface clean and highly readable, paired with Rethink Sans to add a bit of personality.

Colors

I chose warmer background colors to make the product feel more approachable and friendly. I also incorporated semantic colors to convey meaning at a glance, effective for skimming.

THE SOLUTION

Introducing MyFreelance

01 / LAYOUT

See your business at a glance

Freelancers juggle projects, clients, and finances. The dashboard surfaces essentials, including active work, earnings, and tasks, so users instantly understand their financial health without digging through menus.

02 / COLLABORATION MANAGEMENT

Streamline collaboration and referrals

Clicking a request opens a side panel with project details, client info, and a proposed budget. Users must open the details before they can Accept, Refer, or Decline — intentional friction to encourage thoughtful action.

The Refer option serves to build community on the platform by allowing freelancers pass along projects they can't take, fostering trust and reciprocity within their network.

03 / PERSONAL TASK MANAGEMENT

Stay organized, your way

A simple toggle allows users to switch between List view for detailed sorting and Calendar view for a visual schedule, helping them stay organized in their preferred workflow.

THE LONG-TERM VISION

Expanding MyFreelance beyond the dashboard

Although I only had time to design the key screens, I also considered how MyFreelance could evolve as a business to enable more client discovery and growth.

1.

"Book Me" links

Freelancers can add a "Book Me" button to their site or bio, leading to a MyFreelance-hosted profile where clients can request work directly

2.

Subscription tiers

Paid plans offering curated client networks, mentorship programs for skill development, and training modules on business growth and workflow optimization

3.

Smart time tracking

A built-in timer that connects tracked hours to earnings insights — helping freelancers identify their most profitable projects and optimize their time
