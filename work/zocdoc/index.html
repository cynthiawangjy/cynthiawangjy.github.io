Custom messaging templates

Giving providers more control and flexibility in patient communication

I self-initiated and scoped this entire project after uncovering a gap in Zocdoc's provider-patient communication tools. Fully-customizable messaging didn't yet exist on the platform, and after digging into our UXR repository, I found rich prior research to build on.

I owned the project end-to-end from setting my own timeline, auditing the interface, synthesizing research, and designing the complete solution. I presented my work to the entire Design and Research team along with the VP of Design. The project was met with enthusiasm, as it brought fresh momentum to a complex challenge the team had struggled to address for years.

SOLUTION

Take control of every patient message

A messaging system that puts providers in charge—see every message, build and schedule targeted templates, and track all patient interactions in one unified timeline.

01 / HIGHLIGHT

No more hidden messages See everything in one place

Access all message types — automated confirmations, manual requests, and more — in one central hub. View, edit, and manage messages sent by Zocdoc or sent directly by you, all from one place.

02 / HIGHLIGHT

Build targeted templates that sent when you want

Create custom templates with dynamic placeholders. Target specific patient groups by visit reason, patient type, or location—then schedule messages to deliver before or after appointments, including post-visit follow-ups.

03 / HIGHLIGHT

Edit existing messages anytime, ready to use

Take control of Zocdoc's pre-written messages. Update the base template in settings to set a new default, or customize the message for individual patients right before sending—without changing your saved template.

04 / HIGHLIGHT

Track all appointment activity per patient

Review complete communication history right from the appointment card. See what's been sent, check scheduled messages, monitor patient responses, and edit or cancel upcoming sends—all from the appointment card.

THE EXISTING RESEARCH

Provider-patient messaging has been a long-standing gap at Zocdoc

19% of cancellations stem from missing information

42% of appointments lack insurance details

Through reviewing existing UXR, I uncovered a critical problem: communication breakdowns are costing Zocdoc appointments—and provider trust. Providers struggle to reach patients for essentials like insurance verification, yet Zocdoc's existing Request to Call button sees limited adoption.

Why? Providers don't trust what they can't see or control.

THE EXISTING RESEARCH

Providers need messaging tools that are flexible, discoverable, and trustworthy

Providers struggle to reach patients for essentials like insurance verification, yet Zocdoc's existing Request to Call button sees limited adoption. Why? Providers don't trust what they can't see or control.

A critical constraint: Zocdoc messaging only works pre-appointment. Once the appointment ends, providers lose access to message patients through the platform—limiting follow-up care coordination and post-visit instructions.

Key takeaways

"If it ain't broke, don't fix it"

At first, I wanted to redesign everything. But as I dug deeper, I began understanding why these decisions were made in the first place — sometimes it's better to patch and build than tearing it down.

Ask questions, leverage your team

Reaching out to people who worked on earlier iterations gave me the context I was missing, unblocked me countless times, and often revealed stronger ways to approach my own work.

You're the expert of your own work

When presenting, no one knows your projects better than you — you've put in the hours, you've done all the thinking — now you just need to trust your work and share it with confidence.
