Custom messaging templates

Giving providers more control and flexibility in patient communication

During the second half of my internship, I initiated this project after uncovering a gap in Zocdoc's provider-patient communication tools. I owned the project end-to-end from scoping, reviewing existing research, and designing the complete solution.

I presented my work to the Design and Research team along with the VP of Design. The project was met with enthusiasm, as it brought fresh momentum to a complex challenge the team had struggled to address for years.

TIMELINE 4 weeks July – August 2025
ROLE Product Design Intern Sole designer, Product thinking, End-to-end design
FOR Self-initiated "Hot Take Project"
WITH HELP FROM 1 Design Mentor 2 Designers 1 Product Manager 1 VP of Design

REVIEWING THE DATA

Communication gaps are costing providers real appointments — and trust in Zocdoc.

Nearly 1 in 5 provider cancellations happen because they can't reach patients for essential information.

Even worse, 40% of appointments are missing insurance details, yet Zocdoc’s “Request to call” button — designed to fix this — is barely used. Why?

THE INITIAL PROBLEM

Providers don’t feel in control of their own communications — and that loss of visibility has broken their trust in the system.

After listening to 8 interviews and auditng the current experience, I uncovered three key pain points.

01 / PAIN POINT

Rigid communication tools

Providers care deeply about maintaining personal relationships with patients, but the product forces them into a one-size-fits-all approach.

Unclear message behavior

THE CORE PROBLEM

Zocdoc’s system takes control away from providers — determining what, when, and how messages are sent.

SETTING UP CONSTRAINTS

Two-way chat wasn’t an option.

Previous research showed providers worried about HIPAA liability and 24/7 availability expectations with open-ended messaging.

This shaped my approach: deliver flexibility and control within structured, one-directional templates.

PROCESS

Classifying existing messages

IMG

A complete map of all existing messages across the appointment lifecycle

Zocdoc's messages fall into two distinct categories: manually user-triggered messages and automated system messages.

The key difference: automated messages are sent to patients based on rules and timing in relation to the appointment, while user-triggered messages are sent on a per-patient basis when providers need specific information.

01 / EXPLORATIONS

SOLUTION

Take control of every patient message

A messaging system that puts providers in charge — see every message, build and schedule targeted templates, and track all patient interactions in one unified timeline.

01 / HIGHLIGHT

No more hidden messages See everything in one place

Access all message types — automated confirmations, manual requests, and more — in one central hub. View, edit, and manage messages sent by Zocdoc or sent directly by you, all from one place.

02 / HIGHLIGHT

Build targeted templates that sent when you want

Create custom templates with dynamic placeholders. Target specific patient groups by visit reason, patient type, or location—then schedule messages to deliver before or after appointments, including post-visit follow-ups.

03 / HIGHLIGHT

Edit existing messages anytime, ready to use

Take control of Zocdoc's pre-written messages. Update the base template in settings to set a new default, or customize the message for individual patients right before sending—without changing your saved template.

04 / HIGHLIGHT

Track all appointment activity per patient

Review complete communication history right from the appointment card. See what's been sent, check scheduled messages, monitor patient responses, and edit or cancel upcoming sends—all from the appointment card.

IMPACT

MVP delivery and project momentum

I delivered a complete MVP solution for a problem that had stalled for over two years. After presenting to the Design and Research team and VP of Design, the work was met with enthusiasm — sparking conversations about future implementation.

Reducing communication-related cancellations

By enabling faster, more personalized communication, providers can resolve missing insurance earlier—reducing day-of delays and administrative burden.

Improving insurance collection rates

Greater transparency and control over automated messages are expected to improve provider trust and usage of outreach tools like Request to Call, which currently appears underutilized despite 22% of cases involving insurance issues.

Increasing provider trust and feature adoption

When providers can see, control, and verify every message sent on their behalf, they're more likely to adopt Zocdoc's communication tools. This transparency should increase usage of underutilized features like Request to Call, particularly for insurance-related outreach.

CONCLUSION

Notes on next steps

Collaborate with engineering and product management to align on business goals and technical limitations, ensuring a clear path forward.

Explore future enhancements
